**Audit Findings**

I ignored the general sloppiness of the CARM definition, as requested. The remaining main issues are these.

1. **The block-recoding lemma is still not strong enough for Theorem 4.**  
   In [lines 352-392](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:352), the lemma proves only that **block-uniform** JDs are preserved. But Theorem 4 concludes that the translated relation is in 5NF in [lines 461-478](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:461). If 5NF quantifies over all JDs, not just declared/block-uniform ones, this does not follow. You need either:
   - explicitly define 5NF here relative only to declared/entailed block-uniform JDs; or
   - strengthen the lemma to show that no non-block-uniform JD can be entailed by the translated schema.

2. **The block-recoding lemma also has a hidden premise.**  
   The proof says a block-uniform JD over \(\widehat R\) collapses to a JD over \(R\) in [lines 376-385](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:376). That is true for dependencies obtained by recoding dependencies of \(R\), but not for an arbitrary JD declared on \(\widehat R\). The lemma should say “every block-uniform JD inherited from \(R\)” or “every block-uniform JD entailed by the recoded schema”.

3. **Theorem 3 still relies on an unstated KG-ER\(_0\) 5NF assumption.**  
   [Lines 326-328](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:326) say the remaining KG-ER\(_0\) relations have no non-key-implied JDs “from the KG-ER\(_0\) definitions”. Section 1 does not state that. The theorem is correct only if KG-ER\(_0\) includes this as a restriction, or if the theorem assumes all non-identity relationship types are already in PJ/NF.

4. **The \(\textsc{Id}\) semantics still uses undefined notation.**  
   [Line 121](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:121) uses \(\rho\) as a renaming operator, but \(\rho\) is not defined; \(\tau\) is the type function. Also \(\ols{i_j}\) is visually defined but semantically not defined as “the opposite binary position”. This affects every theorem because all three transformations depend on the exact identity semantics.

5. **Theorem 3 is now coherent only because of the strong restriction on identifying predicates.**  
   [Line 75](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:75) says \(P_1,\ldots,P_m\) do not appear in any other constraint. Under that restriction, the earlier counterexample with extra keys on identifying predicates disappears. But then [lines 293-311](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:293) discuss “remaining occurrences” and keys on those binary views, which should no longer exist. The proof should be simplified to match the new syntax.

6. **Theorem 3’s 5NF check for \(I_\iota\) proves only the intended identity JD.**  
   [Lines 320-325](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:320) show that  
   \[
   I_\iota=\pi_{C,A_1}I_\iota\bowtie\cdots\bowtie\pi_{C,A_m}I_\iota
   \]
   is implied by \(C\to A_1\cdots A_m\). Good. But if 5NF is read as “every JD semantically holding for the relation variable”, you need to say that this is the only non-key JD introduced by the schema.

7. **The identity-resolving definition is still slightly confused.**  
   [Lines 183-186](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:183) say “there is a constraint \(\iota_K\)” and also \(\mathcal S\models\iota_K\). If \(\iota_K\) is literally a constraint of \(\mathcal S\), entailment is trivial. If it may be derivable, call it an “identity assertion” or “identity formula”, not necessarily a constraint.

8. **Theorem 4’s notation hides the joined identity relation.**  
   [Lines 423-432](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:423) use \(P.1\), \(P.2\), etc., but \(P\) is not a declared symbol; it is the join relation induced by \(\iota_K\). This should be named, e.g. \(P_{\iota_K}\), to make the total/function/injective argument formal.

9. **The decidability theorem in Section 1 is not checkable as stated.**  
   [Lines 189-190](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:189) say “Entailment of KG-ER\(_0\) schemas” is EXPTIME-complete. That is not a precise decision problem. You need to specify whether this is satisfiability, type non-emptiness, type inclusion entailment, constraint implication, or query answering.

10. **Theorem 5 is mostly sound under the forest-presentation assumption.**  
    I do not see a fatal correctness bug in [lines 506-648](/Users/franconi/GIT/KG-ER/CARM/KG-ER-zero.tex:506), assuming the forest presentation really guarantees same-arity/same-domain projection occurrences along inclusion edges. I would make that explicit in the theorem statement, but the construction and inverse maps are otherwise coherent.

**Bottom Line**

The biggest remaining mathematical issue is #1/#2: the block-recoding lemma does not yet justify full 5NF unless 5NF is relativized to block-uniform declared dependencies. Theorem 3 is now safer because identifying predicates are isolated, but the proof should be simplified accordingly. Theorem 5 looks basically correct under its stated forest-presentation assumption.

Theorems 3 & 5 fixed.
